AI News Digest – 5 June 2025

Total stories: 5

Vista CEO on Private Equity Jobs

Vista CEO Robert F. Smith warned that AI could eliminate up to 60% of private equity roles, particularly those in spreadsheet modeling, due diligence, and back-office functions. He emphasized that professionals in the sector will need to retrain and develop new analytical and strategic skills to remain relevant.

Amazon Invests in North Carolina AI Campus

Amazon has committed $10 billion to build a massive data center and AI campus in rural North Carolina. The facility will create at least 1,000 jobs and aims to support AWS’s AI infrastructure while helping turn the region into a technology corridor.

Microsoft Copilot Update

Microsoft’s latest Copilot update includes the ability to generate PowerPoint slides from meeting transcripts, a new Excel chat interface that writes formulas from plain language, and enhanced document citation support in Word. These changes aim to streamline day-to-day workflow tasks using natural language input.

Infosys Launches AI at Roland-Garros

Infosys introduced AI-driven tools at Roland-Garros 2025, including real-time match analysis, fan heatmaps, and a virtual 3D stadium experience. These innovations are designed to enhance how fans interact with tennis content through personalization and immersive data visualization.

OpenAI on Malicious AI Use

OpenAI released a report outlining real-world attempts to misuse AI — including impersonation, phishing, and fraud — and described new partnerships with threat intelligence firms to detect and disrupt these cases. The company emphasized early detection and cross-platform monitoring as key strategies.

🧠 Reminder: How Your Feedback Shapes the Feed

To avoid overfitting or blinkering, your feedback is interpreted in one of three ways:

  • ✅ Useful — show more like this
  • ❌ Not useful — de-prioritize similar framing
  • 🧭 Interesting or new — keep this kind of variety even if it’s not a direct hit

A few wildcard or “curveball” stories will always be included to preserve breadth and discovery. You can use feedback like:

  • 🌀 “Glad this was included, even if not quite right”
  • ⚖️ “Still unsure — let’s keep watching this space”

How We Can Design Around That

  1. Separate “relevance” from “exploration”
    Not all feedback is treated as a signal to exclude or replicate.
    You’ll be able to say:
    - “This was useful — include more like this” ✅
    - “This was off — don’t prioritize it” ❌
    - “This was new to me — not sure yet, but I’m glad it was included” 👀
  2. Keep a quota of wildcards or long-tails
    Each digest can reserve one or two slots for unusual, experimental, or thought-provoking stories.
    These are marked as “🧭 Explorer Picks” or “🌀 Curveballs”.
  3. Tag your feedback as “clarifying,” not “cutting”
    “This wasn’t very useful today, but don’t filter all stories like this out.”
    “This didn’t land, but it’s good to keep variety.”
    That tells the system: learn how to present better, not eliminate the category.
